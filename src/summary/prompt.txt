**TASK:**
Write a comprehensive, factual news article based *strictly* on the provided sources.

**METHODOLOGY (Chain-of-Thought & Chain of Density):**
You must follow a strict **Plan-then-Iterate** process. Do not write the final article immediately.

1. **PHASE 1: ANALYSIS & PLANNING (Internal Monologue - Chain of Thought)**
    * **Fact Extraction & Mapping:** Map specific facts to their source IDs (e.g., "Casualties: 15 [1], 20 [2]").
    * **Consensus & Conflict Check:** * Identify facts supported by multiple sources. 
        * **Constraint:** Quote the exact snippets from the sources that support the consensus.
        * **CRITICAL:** If sources conflict (e.g., different casualty numbers), explicitly note the discrepancy.
        * **Prioritization:** If a conflict exists between an official datum (e.g., police report) and an estimate, prioritize the official datum but note the estimate. Otherwise, report the range, the most authoritative source, or the most recent figure.
    * **Temporal Ordering:** Establish the timeline.

2. **PHASE 2: INITIAL DRAFT (Sparse)**
    * Write an initial, entity-sparse draft of the article based on your plan. 
    * This draft should capture the main story and be relatively verbose, containing little dense information to reach a target of exactly {{ max_word_count }} words.

3. **PHASE 3: DENSIFICATION LOOP (Chain of Density)**
    * Repeat the following 2 steps exactly 3 times:
        * **Step A:** Identify 1-3 informative entities or facts from your Phase 1 plan that are *missing* from the previously generated draft. A missing entity must be relevant to the main story, specific, novel (not in the previous draft), and faithful to the sources.
        * **Step B:** Write a new, denser draft of **identical length** (exactly {{ max_word_count }} words) that covers every detail from the previous draft *plus* the new missing entities. 
        * **To make space:** You must use abstraction, fusion, compression, and the removal of uninformative filler phrases to make room for the new facts without increasing the word count.

**CONSTRAINTS:**
* **Length:** Every draft in Phase 2 and Phase 3 MUST be exactly {{ max_word_count }} words. Do not exceed or fall short of this limit.
* **Formatting:** Use Markdown (## Headers, **Bold** for emphasis).
* **Safety:** Do not invent details (Hallucination check).
* **Clause-Level Citations:** You must place [n] citations immediately after the specific fact, figure, or quote they support in all drafts. Do not group them all at the end of the sentence.
    * *Bad:* The fire started at 9 PM and destroyed the roof [1][2].
    * *Good:* The fire started at 9 PM [1] and eventually destroyed the roof [2].

**NEGATIVE CONSTRAINTS:**
1. Do NOT calculate arithmetic sums of casualties from different reports (risk of double counting).
2. Do NOT synthesize quotes. Quotes must be verbatim from a single source.
3. Do NOT use temporal words like "yesterday" or "today"; use specific dates.
4. Do NOT drop entities from previous drafts during Phase 3. If space cannot be made, add fewer new entities.
